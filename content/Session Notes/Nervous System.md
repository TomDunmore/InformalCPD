# Autonomic Vs somatic
This is what you control vs what you don't [@pilbery2019ambulancecarepractice]. For example, you choose to move your arm but do not choose to beat your heart. Some functions, like blinking and breathing can be done through both!

# Sympathetic vs parasympathetic
Part of the autonomic nervous system, this controls your reaction to stressors (from exercise to work deadlines). Sympathetic is your fight or flight response. It increases BP, HR and stops immune, reproductive and digestive systems. Parasympathetic is your rest and digest system. It works in the background on normal running [@waugh2023rosswilsonanatomy].

# Assessment
Rule of thumb: if there's an issue with behaviour, perception, speech, movement or eyes, there could be an issue with the brain. 
# Pupils
- Should be equal and reactive
- Measurement taken before shining light
- unequal or unreactive pupils could suggest stroke [@jrcalc2020headachejrcalcguideline]
# ACVPU
- Quick and repeatable
- Done of every patient
- Confusion must be new
- [@jefferies2023acvpuscalelevel]
# GCS
- Slower and more in depth
- Only done for patients with suspected stroke, or significantly unwell
- Score between 3 (lowest) and 15 (highest)
- Any confusion counts as confusion, even if existing
- A score of less than 15 suggests and unwell patient
- [@yousuf2025glasgowcomascale]
