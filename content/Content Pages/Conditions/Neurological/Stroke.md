# Time is brain

- Brain tissue death cause by ischaemia (85%) or bleed (10% bleed in brain, 5% [[subarachnoid haemorrhage]]).
- Rapid transfer to specialist hospital is best outcome

- Key features
	- One sided face weakness
	- One sided arm or leg weakness
	- Speech disturbance

# FAST
- Useful for fast identification of strokes in community (where 95% occur)
- Does not rule out stroke!
- You can be querying stroke in a FAST negative patient
- Always note time of onset

# Stroke mimics
- seizures
- syncope
- sepsis
- hypoglycaemia
- migraine
- decompensation of previous stroke
- functional disorders.


# Transient Ischaemic Attack
Same as a stroke, but self resolving
For our purpose, there is no difference between a TIA and stroke.
If TIA is resolved on arrival, recommend A and E to seek specialist care within 24 hours of symptoms (do not let them drive)


# Management
Hospital ASAP

Pre-alert is the biggest difference you can make outside hospital

Useful link: https://cks.nice.org.uk/topics/stroke-tia/diagnosis/assessment/
