# Driving Ambulances
- Response drive: Using emergency warning lights and signals as well as strict road traffic law exemptions to make progress to a destination
- Most common qualification for response ambulance driving in the UK currently Certificate in Emergency Response Ambulance Driving (CERAD)
- Normal Road Conditions: A non-emergency response driving under all road traffic laws
[@qualsafe2025emergencyresponseambulance]

# Exemptions for Ambulances under routine and emergency conditions
[@aace2018emergencyambulanceresponse]
- Stopping on a clearway:  schedule 7(6) [@2016trafficsignsregulations]
- Parking in the ‘zigzag’ area on a Pedestrian Crossing: schedule 14(15) [@2016trafficsignsregulations]
- Parking in areas with Double Yellow, White, or Red Lines Schedule 7(6) [@2016trafficsignsregulations]
- Leave the engine running while the vehicle is unattended: Regulation 107(2)(a) [@1986roadvehiclesconstruction]
- Parking on the offside during the hours of darkness Regulation 101(2)(a) [@1986roadvehiclesconstruction]
- Parking or driving on a cycle track: Section 21(2)(a) [@1988roadtrafficact]
- Parking on a central reservation: Section 19(2) [@1988roadtrafficact]
- Parking at a designated bus stop: schedule 7(6)[@2016trafficsignsregulations]
- Double parking or parking across a dropped curb: Section 85(3) [@2004trafficmanagementact]
# Response Driving exemptions
- Travelling on the incorrect side of a 'keep left' or 'keep right' sign Schedule 3(4) [@2016trafficsignsregulations]
- Exceeding statutory speed limits: Section 87(1)(1) [@1984roadtrafficregulation]
- Treating a red light as a 'give way', including light controlled crossing and temporary lights:  Schedule 14(1) [@2016trafficsignsregulations]
- Use of audible warning instruments at night on a restricted road between 23:30-07:00 hours: Regulation 99(1)(5) and 37(5) [@1986roadvehiclesconstruction]
- Entering a pedestrian precinct: Individual exemptions to traffic regulation orders under section 1 [@1984roadtrafficregulation]
- Stop within a yellow box junction:  schedule 9(7) [@2016trafficsignsregulations]
- Motorway Regulations: Section 16(1)(d) [@1982motorwaystrafficengland]
	- Direction of driving
	- Drive on the carriageway only
	- Restriction of stopping
	- Restriction of reversing
	- Restriction of use of the hard shoulder
	- Restriction on use of central reservation of verge

# There are no additional exemptions to
- Crossing a double white line: Schedule 9(8) [@2016trafficsignsregulations]
- Dangerous Driving
