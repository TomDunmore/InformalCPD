# What
Pathways are used to get patients the 'right treatment at the right time'. Certain patients require specialist hospital interventions not provided by all emergency departments. Therefore, they will be transferred to a specialist centre or unit - even if round the corner from another hospital.

The usage of these pathways are specified by the policies and procedures of the local trust. Find using [[NHS Service Finder]]

# Pathways
## Major Trauma
[@transferpathwayspolicies]
- Used for patients experiencing life threatening trauma
- Involves senior clinical support and specialist teams ([[Helicopter Emergency Medical Services]])

## Primary Percutaneous Coronary Intervention (PPCI)
[@nwas2019ppciprotocolrevised]
- Time critical intervention at hospital for complete ST elevation myocardial infarction (severe heart attack)
- Patients must meet specific criteria to be eligible for this - not all heart attack patients will be eligible.

## Hyper Acute Stroke Unit
[@northbristolnhstrusthyperacutestroke]
- Receiving hospital with specialist assessment and treatment for strokes
- Typically must be currently experiencing symptoms of stroke to be eligible 
## Obstetrics Unit or Emergency Maternity Service
[@greatermanchesterclinicalnetwork2025northwestmaternity]
- Receiving hospital with suitable resources to manage active labour
- Especially important for complications during childbirth
