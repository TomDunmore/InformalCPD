# What is it?
Mouldable mattress used for the immobilisation and carrying of patients with increased comfort and reduced risk of pressure sores due to increased contact area.

# Indications
- Patients requiring immobilisation in the following cases:
	- 'prolonged journey time' [@jrcalc2025spinalinjuryspinal]
	- Ideal to use a vacuum mattress [@nice2016spinalinjuryassessment]
	- All immobilisations (due to time taken to package, transfer and unpackage likely lasting over 45 minutes) [@stjohnambulance2020vacuummattress2020]

# Contraindications (When to avoid)
- Patients over 150kg or who cannot be strapped in
- Scenes with hazards including sharp glass or contaminants

# How to use (as per manual below)
1) Fit straps at appropriate heights for the patient
2) Place patient on vacuum mattress (typically with scoop stretcher)
3) Remove [[Scoop Stretcher]] and form mattress to conform with patients body and head( maintain manual in line stabilisation)
	- Note - Guidance on cervical collars is outside the scope of this document [@jrcalc2025spinalinjuryspinal]. Although the manual below does not recommend head blocks with a vacuum mattress. Some imply you should [@jrcalc2025spinalinjuryspinal; @nice2016spinalinjuryassessment]. The goal is to immobilise the head and may vary for type of patient or mattress.
4) Use provided pump to withdraw air from mattress (LSU suction unit *can* be used if not available), and lock the valve
5) Resecure straps

# Use and removal
- Follow normal moving and handling and use provided hand holds
- The mattress is not  designed to slide
- To be removed at hospital, control c-spine and deflate mattress
- If c-spine not cleared, lift off mattress with scoop-stretcher
