# What is it?
Used to remove blockages from the airway from blood, vomit or secretions.
There are two types of suction catheters, attached with an intermediary catheter (seen with rigid below)
- Rigid (Yankauer)

![[Yankauer.png | 400]]
- Flexible

![[Flexible Catheter.png| 400]]
# Important notes
- Postural drainage is often more effective [@pilbery2019ambulancecarepractice]
- Suctioning removes air, keep short and give oxygen before (preoxygenate) and after
- Limit suction to 15 seconds
- If airway is completely blocked, first focus on at least partially clearing before oxygenating [@pilbery2019ambulancecarepractice]
- Only suction what you can see
- Suction on the way out
# Instructions for Use 
1) Prepare equipment, PPE and preoxygenate
2) Attach catheter needed and turn dial to suction power needed
	- 150-200mmhg for small amounts, otherwise start at max and work down
3) Insert catheter making sure you can always see the end of the catheter
4) Cover the control vent on the catheter to start suction
	- Gently withdraw whilst suctioning
	- Limit suction to 15 seconds
5) Reoxygenate and reassess

# Perform Test
1) Cover suction valve, do not uncover until complete
2) Hold 'test' button and turn dial to max
3) wait for each light to turn green and go back to the lowest
4) uncover valve, press 'test' button to cycle lights, all should be green
![LSU Device Test - YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gSW_8Od4Yv0)
