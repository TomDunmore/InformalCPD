# Bilateral Tonic Clonic Seizures (BLTCS)
- Caused by brain irritation (those with epilepsy more susceptible)
- Is a symptom, not just a condition
- Both sided (bilateral)
- Stiffening (tonic)
- Shaking (clonic)
- Unresponsive during seizure
- Typically self resolves in 90 seconds
- Patients will be drowsy (post-ictal after)
- Focal seizures lasting over 60 minutes should be treated the same
- If there is ever a care plan, follow it over general guidance

# Functional / Dissociative Seizures
- AKA FND Non-epileptic attack disorder (NEAD), psychogenic seizures  or conversion disorder
- Do not use the term pseudo-seizure, the seizure is real.
- Functional seizures are far more common than malingered (acted out) seizures
- Last prolonged periods, but no long term risk is present
- Very important to distinguish between BLTCS

# Syncope (Fainting)
- A short amount of seizure activity can be seen in those who faint
- Lasts about 20 seconds with quick recovery
- This is normal and has no long term effects when circulation is resolved

# Assessment
- History, history history!
- Full observations
- Detail and record exact nature, timing of seizures

# Management
- A-E approach
- If there is a care plan, follow that first
- Functional seizures should be managed by reassurance assuming the patient can hear you, does not typically need oxygen
- Active BLTCS require 15L/m oxygen and airway management/suction (mindful of lockjaw or trismus)
- Consider need for medication / HCP support if not resolving
- 5 minutes or more is considered status epilepticus and is when medication is used
